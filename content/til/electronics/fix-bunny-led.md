This effectively amounted to replacing a battery but I still learned some things along the way.

It was thankfully easy to take the rubber case off which I expected to be glued or something but I just had to pull it off.

This revealed the main board with the LEDs and the battery tucked underneath.

![base of light with cover removed](/til/electronics/fix-bunny-led-cover-removed.webp)

It turned out to be a 18650 lithium battery.

![base disassembled showing the battery connected](/til/electronics/fix-bunny-led-battery.webp)

> Adam Savage has since released a [video about 18650 batteries](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-Y23nfAOiXQ) which probably explains why it stopped working.

You'll get a lot of options when searching for 18650 batteries but the tricky part was finding one with the right connector.

![jst connector of the battery](/til/electronics/fix-bunny-led-jst-connector.webp)

[This replacement worked](https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0CGTYDJLM)
